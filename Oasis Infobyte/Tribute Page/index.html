ABOUT

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national cricket team. He was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India.

Dhoni made his international debut in December 2004, and he quickly established himself as one of the most talented and successful cricketers of his generation. He is known for his calm demeanor on the field, his exceptional leadership skills, and his outstanding performance as a wicket-keeper and middle-order batsman.

Dhoni is considered one of the greatest cricket captains in history. He led the Indian team to many memorable victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. He is also the only captain to have won all three major ICC trophies (World Cup, World T20, and Champions Trophy).

Dhoni retired from international cricket in August 2020, after a highly successful career that spanned more than 15 years. He is also a successful businessman and has been associated with several brands as their ambassador. Dhoni is widely admired and respected not just for his cricketing achievements, but also for his humble and down-to-earth personality.

AWARDS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

  • Padma Bhushan:

    In 2018, Dhoni was awarded India's third-highest civilian award, the Padma Bhushan, for his contribution to Indian cricket.

  • ICC ODI Player of the Year:

    Dhoni has won the ICC ODI Player of the Year award twice, in 2008 and 2009.

  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna:

    Dhoni was awarded India's highest sporting honor, the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, in 2007.

  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World

    Dhoni was named Wisden's Leading Cricketer in the World for the years 2008 and 2009.

  • ICC Spirit of Cricket Award:

    In 2011, Dhoni was awarded the ICC Spirit of Cricket Award for his exemplary conduct on the field.

  • Limca Book of Records:

    Dhoni was featured in the Limca Book of Records for the most international matches as captain of a national cricket team, with 332 matches across all formats.

  • IPL Success:

    Dhoni has been a part of three IPL-winning teams as captain of the Chennai Super Kings, in 2010, 2011, and 2018.

  • Captaincy Records:

    Dhoni holds several captaincy records in international cricket, including the most wins by an Indian captain in Tests and ODIs, and the most matches as captain of the Indian cricket team across all formats.

  • World Cup Winning Captain:

    Dhoni led India to victory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, becoming the second Indian captain to achieve this feat after Kapil Dev in 1983.

  • ICC Champions Trophy Winning Captain:

    Dhoni also led India to victory in the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, making him the first captain to win all three major ICC tournaments (World Cup, World T20, and Champions Trophy).
